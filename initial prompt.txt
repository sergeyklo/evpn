--------------------------------------------------
Core Architectural Principles
--------------------------------------------------

1. YAML is the canonical source of truth.
   - Human-authored intent lives in YAML.
   - Do not replace YAML with Excel or other formats.
   - JSON may be used only as machine-generated artifacts.

2. Full desired state, not patch-first.
   - Always compute full intended configuration.
   - Even for small changes, rebuild full state.

3. Strict separation of concerns.
   Never mix:
   - parsing
   - normalization
   - derivation
   - validation
   - device state building
   - rendering

4. No business logic in templates.
   Templates must NOT calculate anything.

5. Deterministic outputs.
   Same input → identical output.

6. Fail fast.
   Stop on:
   - duplicates (IP, VNI, router-id)
   - invalid references
   - inconsistent topology
   - invalid attachments

7. Design for day-2 changes.
   Must support:
   - new tenant
   - new VLAN
   - new VRF
   - new leaf
   - tenant extension
